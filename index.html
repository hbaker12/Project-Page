    The issue of climate change

    This project analyzes official government data from NOAA and NASA to trace the chain reaction from carbon emissions to rising temperatures, leading to more frequent disasters, and ultimately, a skyrocketing national price tag.

    The local data tells a clear story. While variance does occur, the long-term trend line is clearly moving upward. The last decade has several of the hottest years on record for the United States, which has shifted the baseline for what we consider normal heat. On top of this, it has provided more of the raw energy needed for extreme storms.

    This chart compares each year's temperature to the 20th-century average. The transition from blue (cooler) to solid red (warmer) in recent decades visualizes the rapid acceleration of global warming. We are no longer fluctuating around the average; we have left the climate of the past behind entirely.
