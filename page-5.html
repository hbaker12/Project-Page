    Quality Over Quantity

    Not all disasters are made equal. The largest costs faced by the US and other countries come from a few mega disasters

    The average cost per disaster is going up ever so slightly in recent years. Even if we stopped the number of storms from increasing, the price of these storms would still rise because the storms we get are stronger, and are hitting cities that are more crowded than before.

    Tropical Cyclones (Hurricanes) clearly create the most expensive damage to our nation. Severe storms happen consistently all across the US, but Hurricanes deal the worst blows to the economy, accounting for more than 50% of the expenses generated from natural disasters. Droughts are in third place, slowly destroying agriculture in the background, something that many people do not know.
